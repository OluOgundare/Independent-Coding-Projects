A simple moving average (SMA) is an arithmetic moving average calculated by adding recent prices and then dividing by the number of days in the calculation period. For example, one could add the closing price of a security for a number of time periods and then divide this total by that same number of periods. Short-term averages respond quickly to changes in the price of the underlying security, while long-term averages are slower to react. The SMA evaluates the true price (the average of the high, low, and closing prices) for each day. s.

A Bollinger Band is a technical analysis tool defined by a set of trendlines. They are plotted as a certain number of standard deviations (for the determined period) above and below the simple moving average (SMA) of a security's price. Since the width of the bands is based on standard deviation, they adjust to volatility swings in the underlying price. When the price of a stock hits either the upper or lower band, this may be interpreted as a signal to sell or buy.
